Natural Control for
Real Golf Courses

Golf courses are not simple environments. There are paths, slopes, crossings, and clear expectations about where and how a cart should move.

POINGO focuses on one simple idea: people should be able to tell a cart what they want in a natural way, and the system should take responsibility for executing that intent appropriately on the course.

Users point to express intent, then confirm to authorize action. From there, the system manages motion details such as stability, speed, and course-aware behavior, resulting in calmer and more predictable movement during play.

What We’re Building

POINGO is building a unified control system for golf mobility, designed around how people naturally interact with carts on real courses.

  • When users step in, control is simple and direct: point, confirm, done.
  • When the cart operates on its own, the system decides how to move in a way that fits the course.

The same control logic applies to walking push carts and low-speed golf carts, keeping behavior consistent across different products and use cases.

  • One consistent control language
  • Clear intent and authorization
  • Calmer behavior on slopes and paths
  • Designed for real course expectations
