Before anything else, this is what POINGO is not: we’re not trying to build carts, and we’re not trying to replace what great manufacturers already do well.

We focus on one thing — how people naturally tell a walking cart (or a low-speed golf cart) what to do, on a real golf course. Golf courses aren’t simple environments: paths, slopes, crossings, areas you shouldn’t enter, and speed expectations. So “follow” isn’t just tracking a person — it’s about respecting the course.

That’s why POINGO uses a simple flow: point to express intent, then confirm to authorize action. The system handles the details — stability, speed control, and safer behavior on slopes and uneven ground.

What We’re Building

We don’t see “follow” as a feature. To us, follow is just one outcome of a system that understands the course and the situation.

  • When a user steps in — control stays natural: point, confirm, done. No continuous steering. No babysitting.
  • When the cart is following — the system decides path, speed, and how to avoid things based on the course — not the user.

This same control philosophy can power both walking push carts and low-speed golf carts, while keeping behavior calmer and more predictable in real terrain.

  • One simple control language across modes
  • Clear “intent → authorization → execution” boundary
  • Better behavior on slopes, paths, and crossings
  • Designed for golf-course expectations, not lab conditions

Technology Overview

Point & Confirm

Pointing expresses intent. Confirmation authorizes action (pattern can vary by context), so movement is never accidental.

Calmer Manual Control

Manual shouldn’t mean micro-steering. The system stabilizes motion and manages speed so users don’t carry the burden.

Smarter Course Behavior

Following adapts to terrain and obstacles. The goal is predictable behavior that fits real course expectations.
