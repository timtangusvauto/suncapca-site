Intent-Based Control — Point, Confirm, Execute

Intent-Based Control for
Golf Course Mobility

POINGO is a patent-pending, intent-based control system developed by California Suncap LLC. It changes how golfers interact with mobility devices — from button-driven operation to expressing intent through pointing and confirmation.

Golfers point to express direction or behavior intent, then confirm to authorize execution. From there, the system handles motion details — including stability, speed, obstacle awareness, and terrain-appropriate behavior — on real golf courses with slopes, paths, and uneven ground.

What We’re Building

POINGO is building a unified, intent-based control platform designed specifically for golf-course environments.

  • Smart Follow Push Cart — decision-aware following that adapts to terrain, obstacles, and course behavior.
  • Intent-Based Manual & Terrain-Aware Golf Cart — assisted manual control with system-managed stability and safety.

The same core system interprets user intent through pointing and confirmation, then translates that intent into smooth, constrained, and context-aware vehicle behavior across different modes.

  • Minimal, learnable handheld interaction
  • Point to express intent, confirm to authorize action
  • Assisted manual control (system-managed stability & safety)
  • Decision-aware smart follow (terrain + obstacles + course behavior)

Technology Overview

Point & Confirm Interaction

Intent is expressed through natural pointing, then authorized through context-dependent confirmation.

Assisted Manual Control

Even in manual mode, the system manages stability, speed, and basic obstacle awareness to reduce user burden.

Course-Aware Decision Layer

Movement decisions are informed by terrain profiles, obstacles, and golf-course behavior rules.
