Control intent Basic follow or basic remote commands. Limited understanding of user intent. Directional intent control. The user’s pointing direction directly defines cart motion. Intent interpreted and translated into safe, rule-aware motion planning.
Follow behavior Works in open conditions. Can degrade with turns, slopes, or partial signal loss. Stable follow tuned for walking pace, with predictable distance and response. Course-aware follow behavior that adapts to terrain and operational context.
Terrain handling Limited slope awareness. Some systems slow down without explaining why. Slope-informed speed limits for comfort and stability. Terrain-based path selection, speed profiling, and risk scoring.
Safety logic Mostly reactive. Stop when close to an obstacle. Multi-layer safety: speed control, stop logic, and clear user feedback. Predictive safety: slow zones, no-go areas, and safer turning envelopes.
Rule awareness Generally not rule-aware. Users manually avoid restricted areas. Conservative behavior designed to remain within typical course expectations. Integrated rule engine with geofencing and course-specific constraints.
User workload Frequent corrections required as conditions change. Low workload with predictable, learnable control behavior. Near hands-off operation within permitted zones.
Upgrade path Feature-limited. Difficult to extend beyond the original design. Software-centric platform with expandable capabilities. Full-stack roadmap: mapping, diagnostics, and course operations integration.

“Mainstream market today” reflects typical consumer follow and remote carts under real course conditions. POINGO Pro refers to an upgrade on the same hardware platform through additional software, mapping, and rule-aware logic. POINGO® is a registered trademark.
